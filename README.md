Olist E-Commerce Analysis

Why Are Customers Unhappy — and Why Don't They Come Back?

A data analysis case study investigating customer dissatisfaction and low retention on Olist, a real Brazilian e-commerce marketplace.


Tools Used:
BigQuery SQL — data cleaning and analysis
Tableau Public — dashboard and visualizations
Google Sheets — data exports and summaries

Dataset
Source: Kaggle — Brazilian E-Commerce Public Dataset by Olist
Period: September 2016 – October 2018
Size: 100,000+ orders across 6 relational tables

Business Problem:
Olist is experiencing low customer retention. This project investigates the root causes of poor customer satisfaction and identifies how negative experiences impact repeat purchase behaviour.

Business Questions:
What drives low review scores?
Which product categories receive the most negative reviews?
What percentage of customers place a second order?
Do customers with poor experiences return less often?
What is the estimated revenue lost due to poor customer experience?

Key Findings:
Late delivery drives bad reviews — 36.62% of 1-star orders were late vs only 1.86% of 5-star orders
Worst categories — fashion male clothing (28.24%), office furniture (26.08%), and fixed telephony (25.57%) have the highest % of negative reviews
Only 3% of customers return — 97% of customers never placed a second order
Happy customers return twice as often — 2.70% return rate for 5-star vs 1.375% for unhappy customers
$26,602 estimated revenue lost — conservative minimum due to poor customer experience

Recommendations:
Improve delivery speed and reliability
Investigate and fix underperforming product categories
Prioritize bed_bath_table and furniture_decor — highest volume of unhappy customers
Invest in customer retention programs (loyalty incentives, follow-up emails)
Address the $26,602+ revenue gap through targeted retention efforts
